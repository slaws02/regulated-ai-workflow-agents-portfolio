TEXAS STATE HEALTHCARE MANDATE BASELINE

Texas health plans must comply with state-mandated benefit requirements published by the Texas Department of Insurance (TDI). These mandates apply to state-regulated plans and may vary by market segment.

SOURCE: Texas Department of Insurance Mandated Benefits Chart


2025 LEGISLATIVE SESSION — PASSED UPDATES (AFFECTING PLAN REQUIREMENTS)

SB 896 — Newborn Coverage Enrollment Window
Newborn automatic enrollment period extended from 31 days to 60 days.
Effective January 1, 2026.
Applies to Texas-regulated health plans.

HB 388 — Coordination of Benefits Standardization
Requires standardized coordination-of-benefits questionnaire.
Effective February 1, 2026.
Applies to carriers and administrators.


2025 REGULATORY & OPERATIONAL IMPACT LAWS

HB 1612 — Direct Provider Payment Acceptance
Hospitals must accept direct patient payment in certain cases.
Impacts billing practices.

HB 3812 — Prior Authorization Reform
Limits prior authorization use and expands physician override authority.
Impacts utilization review processes.


INDIVIDUAL COVERAGE MANDATE STATUS

Texas does NOT have a state-level individual health insurance mandate requirement for residents.
